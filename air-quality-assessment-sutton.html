Environmental Consultants in Sutton

Sutton is one of south London's most attractive residential boroughs, with a strong town centre at Sutton itself, the picturesque Carshalton Ponds conservation area, and the leafy suburbs of Cheam and Worcester Park. The borough is seeing growing development pressure, particularly around Sutton town centre where regeneration proposals include significant new residential and commercial floorspace.

The borough sits within the Greater London Air Quality Management Area and the ULEZ. While generally having better air quality than inner London boroughs, key road corridors including the A217 Brighton Road, A232 and A24 create localised air quality issues that must be considered for development nearby. Sutton Council applies GLA guidance in assessing air quality impacts of planning applications.

Air Dust Odour provides air quality assessments, dust management plans and odour impact assessments across Sutton. As Chartered Environmentalists and MIAQM members, we prepare reports to the standards required by Sutton Council and the Greater London Authority.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need an air quality assessment in Sutton?

Sutton falls within the Greater London Air Quality Management Area and the ULEZ. Most planning applications for residential, commercial or mixed-use development will require an air quality assessment. Key road corridors including the A217, A232 and A24 create air quality constraints near development sites, and Sutton Council expects applicants to assess air quality impacts in accordance with GLA and national guidance.

What areas of Sutton do you cover?

We cover all areas of the London Borough of Sutton including Sutton town centre, Carshalton, Cheam, Wallington, Belmont, Benhilton, Worcester Park, Stonecot Hill and surrounding neighbourhoods. We are familiar with the borough's planning policies and air quality requirements.

How much does an air quality assessment cost in Sutton?

Costs depend on the type and complexity of assessment required. Screening assessments typically start from around £500, detailed air quality assessments with dispersion modelling from around £1,500, and kitchen odour assessments from around £800. Contact us for a free, no-obligation quote tailored to your project.

Do I need a dust assessment for construction in Sutton?

Yes. Sutton Council requires a Construction Environmental Management Plan (CEMP) or dust assessment for most developments involving demolition, excavation or construction in sensitive areas. We prepare CEMPs in line with IAQM guidance on the assessment of dust from demolition and construction.

We are opening a restaurant in Sutton town centre — do we need an odour assessment?

Yes, most new or significantly altered food premises in Sutton will require an odour impact assessment as part of the planning application. We prepare assessments in accordance with EMAQ+ guidance on the assessment and rating of odour from commercial kitchens, and can advise on appropriate extraction and filtration to meet council requirements.
