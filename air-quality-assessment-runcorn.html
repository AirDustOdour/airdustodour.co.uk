Environmental Consultants in Runcorn

Runcorn sits on the south bank of the River Mersey within Halton Borough Council, connected to Widnes by the Silver Jubilee Bridge and the Mersey Gateway crossing. The town and its surroundings host one of the UK's largest chemical and petrochemical clusters at Weston Point, with major operators including Ineos and Tronox. This industrial concentration creates some of the most complex air quality, odour and dust assessment challenges in the north-west of England, requiring specialist expertise for planning applications across the borough.

The M56 and A557 motorway corridors, including the Mersey crossing approaches, generate elevated nitrogen dioxide concentrations across a wide area. Halton Borough Council's significant industrial land bank — particularly around the Runcorn waterfront and Weston Point — is subject to ongoing pressure for redevelopment, with proposed residential and mixed-use schemes routinely requiring odour impact assessment to demonstrate acceptability against the backdrop of permitted industrial operations.

Air Dust Odour provides air quality assessments, odour impact assessments and dust management plans for development across Runcorn, Widnes and the wider Halton area. As Chartered Environmentalists and MIAQM members, we prepare assessments to the standards required by Halton Borough Council and the Environment Agency, including industrial odour assessments under H4 guidance.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need an air quality assessment in Runcorn?

Halton Borough Council may require an air quality assessment for planning applications near declared Air Quality Management Areas, the M56/A557 motorway corridors, or the Runcorn chemical cluster at Weston Point. Any development that introduces new sensitive receptors near major roads or industrial sources, or that generates significant additional traffic, is likely to require assessment.

What are the main air quality issues in Runcorn?

Runcorn's principal air quality concerns centre on the chemical and petrochemical cluster at Weston Point, one of the UK's largest, which operates under Environmental Permits issued by the Environment Agency. The M56 and A557 Mersey crossing approach roads are significant sources of elevated nitrogen dioxide. Halton Borough's industrial land bank, particularly around the waterfront and Weston Point, generates ongoing odour and dust assessment requirements for redevelopment proposals.

How much does an air quality assessment cost in Runcorn?

Costs depend on the type and complexity of assessment required. Screening assessments typically start from around £500, detailed air quality assessments with dispersion modelling from around £1,500, odour impact assessments from around £1,200, and dust risk assessments from around £800. Do I need an odour assessment for development near the Runcorn chemical cluster?

Yes. Residential or mixed-use development proposed in proximity to Weston Point and the wider Runcorn chemical cluster will typically require an odour impact assessment to demonstrate that future occupants will not be exposed to unacceptable odour levels. We assess odour using IAQM and Environment Agency guidance, including H4 Odour Management.

Do I need a dust assessment for construction in Runcorn?

Yes. Halton Borough Council expects a Construction Environmental Management Plan (CEMP) or dust risk assessment for development involving demolition, excavation or construction near residential properties or other sensitive receptors. We prepare dust assessments and CEMPs in accordance with IAQM guidance on the assessment of dust from demolition and construction.
