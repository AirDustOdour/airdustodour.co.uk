Malcolm Pounder

CEnv · MIAQM · MIEnvSc

Chartered Environmentalist and founder of Air Dust Odour. Over a decade of experience in air quality, dust and odour assessment for UK planning applications. Newcastle University alumnus, based in County Durham.

Malcolm Pounder is a Chartered Environmentalist (CEnv) registered with the Society for the Environment, a Member of the Institute of Air Quality Management (MIAQM), and a Member of the Institution of Environmental Sciences (MIEnvSc). He is the founder of Air Dust Odour, an environmental consultancy based in County Durham providing air quality, dust and odour assessments to clients across the United Kingdom.

Malcolm read for his degree at Newcastle University and has spent his career in the environmental sciences, latterly specialising in air quality assessment for planning. Before founding Air Dust Odour he held a principal consultant role with a UK acoustics and air quality practice, leading air quality projects on behalf of national-scale clients. He founded Air Dust Odour to deliver chartered-quality environmental consultancy with the responsiveness and clarity that smaller projects rarely get from larger firms.

What Malcolm Leads On

Malcolm leads every Air Dust Odour project personally — scoping, modelling, fieldwork and report writing. His practice covers:

  • Operational-phase air quality assessments using ADMS-Roads, ADMS-Urban and AERMOD dispersion models for residential, commercial and industrial schemes across the UK
  • Construction-phase dust impact assessments under the IAQM 2024 V2.2 framework, with CEMPs, NRMM compliance strategies and condition-discharge support
  • Odour assessments — qualitative IAQM-framework, EMAQ+ kitchen extract, and quantitative dispersion modelling for agricultural, industrial and hospitality sources
  • Air Quality Neutral and AQ Positive assessments for London Plan Policy SI 1 compliance on major London schemes
  • Diffusion tube monitoring for NO2 baseline establishment, model verification and post-construction compliance, with UKAS-accredited laboratory analysis
  • Workplace air quality surveys for COSHH compliance, with samples analysed by UKAS-accredited specialist laboratories such as IOM
  • EIA-grade air quality chapters and committee-level support for contested applications
