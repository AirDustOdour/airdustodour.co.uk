Environmental Consultants in Harlow

Harlow is an Essex new town positioned at the heart of one of the UK's most significant strategic growth corridors. As part of the London-Stansted-Cambridge arc, the town is set for transformative expansion: the Harlow and Gilston Garden Town programme envisions up to 16,000 new homes alongside new schools, transport infrastructure and commercial development. Meanwhile, the Harlow Enterprise Zone continues to attract investment from the pharmaceutical and life science sectors, building on Harlow's long-established reputation as a hub for science and innovation. This scale of growth requires careful, expert management of air quality and related environmental matters throughout the planning and development process.

Harlow District Council has declared Air Quality Management Areas along the A414 and A1025 corridors, where monitoring and modelling has demonstrated that national objectives for nitrogen dioxide are exceeded. These roads carry heavy volumes of commuter and freight traffic, and the planned growth in the area will place additional demands on the local network. Developers bringing forward residential, commercial or mixed-use schemes within or near the AQMAs must demonstrate through robust air quality assessment that their proposals will not worsen existing conditions, and that new sensitive receptors are not exposed to unacceptable pollutant concentrations.

Air Dust Odour provides air quality, dust and odour assessments for planning applications across Harlow and the surrounding area. Our Chartered Environmentalists understand the requirements of Harlow District Council and the strategic planning context of the Garden Town, and we prepare assessments that are authoritative, clearly reasoned and built to withstand scrutiny from planning officers and statutory consultees. Whether you are a major housebuilder, a pharmaceutical developer or a small business operator, we can provide the environmental consultancy support you need.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need an air quality assessment in Harlow?

Harlow District Council has declared Air Quality Management Areas covering the A414 and A1025 corridors where nitrogen dioxide concentrations have historically exceeded national air quality objectives. Any development proposed within or close to these AQMAs, or that would generate significant additional traffic on the local road network, is likely to require an air quality assessment as part of the planning application. The scale of housing and commercial growth planned through the Harlow and Gilston Garden Town means that air quality considerations are central to the strategic planning framework for the area.

What is the Harlow and Gilston Garden Town and how does it affect air quality requirements?

The Harlow and Gilston Garden Town is one of the UK's most ambitious planned growth programmes, with up to 16,000 new homes planned across sites in and around Harlow and in the East Herts district to the north. This level of development brings with it significant infrastructure requirements and cumulative increases in traffic that must be assessed in terms of their air quality implications. Developers working on sites within the Garden Town framework should expect detailed air quality assessments to be required, often incorporating strategic transport modelling and cumulative impact analysis across the combined development trajectory.

Do pharmaceutical and science sector developments at Harlow Enterprise Zone need air quality assessments?

Yes. Commercial and research developments at the Harlow Enterprise Zone, which has attracted major pharmaceutical and life science operators, may require air quality assessment depending on their scale and potential emission sources. Industrial processes, laboratory operations and significant traffic generation associated with enterprise zone development all carry potential air quality implications. We work with developers and operators to prepare the assessments needed to support planning applications and, where relevant, Environmental Permit applications.

Do I need a dust assessment for construction in Harlow?

Yes. Construction projects in Harlow that involve demolition, significant earthworks or extended construction programmes near sensitive receptors will require a dust risk assessment and, for larger schemes, a Construction Environmental Management Plan. Harlow's established residential neighbourhoods sit in close proximity to many regeneration and growth sites, making robust dust management essential during construction. We prepare assessments in accordance with IAQM guidance, identifying risks and specifying proportionate mitigation measures.

We are opening a restaurant or food business in Harlow — do we need an odour assessment?

Yes. New or significantly expanded commercial kitchen operations in Harlow will typically require an odour impact assessment as part of the planning process, particularly where the premises is adjacent to or above residential uses. We prepare kitchen odour assessments using the EMAQ+ methodology, which is the industry-accepted standard for planning applications, and provide practical advice on extraction system specification and abatement technology to support a successful outcome.
