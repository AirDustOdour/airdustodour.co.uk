Environmental Consultants in Luton

Luton is one of the South East's most important commercial centres, home to a major international airport and a significant manufacturing and logistics sector. The town is undergoing substantial regeneration, with major development proposals for the town centre and the continued expansion of London Luton Airport generating new development activity across the area.

Luton has declared Air Quality Management Areas where national objectives for nitrogen dioxide are not met. The M1 motorway, A505 and the town centre road network, combined with emissions from airport operations and associated road traffic, create a complex air quality environment. Luton Borough Council expects air quality to be robustly assessed for all significant planning applications.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need an air quality assessment in Luton?

Luton Borough Council has declared Air Quality Management Areas covering significant parts of the town where national objectives for nitrogen dioxide are exceeded. The M1, A505 and the town centre road network are key sources of traffic-related air pollution. Most planning applications for residential, commercial or mixed-use development in or near an AQMA will require an air quality assessment.

Does Luton Airport affect air quality assessments in Luton?

Yes. London Luton Airport is a significant source of air pollution in the area, contributing to elevated background concentrations of nitrogen dioxide and particulate matter particularly in areas beneath flight paths and near the airport perimeter. Aircraft and airport-related road traffic must be considered in air quality assessments for developments in the vicinity of the airport.

How much does an air quality assessment cost in Luton?

Costs depend on the type and complexity of assessment required. Screening assessments typically start from around £500, detailed air quality assessments with dispersion modelling from around £1,500, and kitchen odour assessments from around £800. Contact us for a free, no-obligation quote.

Do I need a dust assessment for construction in Luton?

Yes. Luton Borough Council requires a Construction Environmental Management Plan (CEMP) or dust assessment for most developments involving demolition, excavation or construction near sensitive receptors. We prepare CEMPs in accordance with IAQM guidance.

We are opening a food business in Luton — do we need an odour assessment?

Yes, most new or significantly altered food premises in Luton will require an odour impact assessment as part of the planning application. We prepare assessments in accordance with EMAQ+ guidance and can advise on appropriate extraction and filtration systems.
