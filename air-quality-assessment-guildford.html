Environmental Consultants in Guildford

Guildford is Surrey's county town and one of the South East's most prosperous commercial centres. The town is subject to significant development pressure, with major proposals for the town centre and continued growth in the surrounding area. Guildford Borough Council's Local Plan identifies substantial housing and commercial development needs over the coming years.

Guildford has an Air Quality Management Area centred on the town centre and the A3 corridor. The town's valley location limits the natural dispersion of traffic-related pollutants, and the A3 trunk road — one of the busiest roads in the South East — generates elevated nitrogen dioxide concentrations along its length through the town. The council requires air quality assessment for planning applications that could affect or be affected by the AQMA.

Air Dust Odour provides air quality assessments, dust management plans and odour impact assessments across Guildford and Surrey. As Chartered Environmentalists and MIAQM members, we prepare reports to the standards required by Guildford Borough Council.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need an air quality assessment in Guildford?

Guildford Borough Council has declared an Air Quality Management Area covering the town centre and the A3 corridor where national objectives for nitrogen dioxide are exceeded. Most planning applications for residential, commercial or mixed-use development in or near the AQMA will require an air quality assessment. Developments that generate significant additional traffic may also require assessment outside the AQMA.

What are the main air quality issues in Guildford?

Guildford's air quality challenges are primarily driven by the A3 trunk road passing through and around the town, the busy town centre gyratory, and the High Street which has historically been a pollution hotspot. The town's location in a valley restricts dispersion of pollutants. Guildford Borough Council takes air quality seriously in planning decisions, particularly for developments near the A3 and in the AQMA.

How much does an air quality assessment cost in Guildford?

Costs depend on the type and complexity of assessment required. Screening assessments typically start from around £500, detailed air quality assessments with dispersion modelling from around £1,500, and kitchen odour assessments from around £800. Contact us for a free, no-obligation quote.

Do I need a dust assessment for construction in Guildford?

Yes. Guildford Borough Council requires a Construction Environmental Management Plan (CEMP) or dust assessment for most developments involving demolition, excavation or construction near sensitive receptors. We prepare CEMPs in accordance with IAQM guidance.

We are opening a restaurant in Guildford town centre — do we need an odour assessment?

Yes, most new or significantly altered food premises in Guildford will require an odour impact assessment as part of the planning application, particularly in the town centre where residential properties are above or adjacent to commercial uses. We prepare assessments in accordance with EMAQ+ guidance.
