Environmental Consultants in Hackney

Hackney is one of London's most dynamic and densely populated boroughs, stretching from Shoreditch and Hoxton in the south to Stoke Newington in the north. With Hackney Wick emerging as a major regeneration and creative district, and ongoing development pressure across the borough, robust environmental assessment has never been more important.

The borough sits within the Greater London Air Quality Management Area and the Ultra Low Emission Zone. Key roads including the A10 (Stoke Newington High Street), A107 and the A12 at Hackney Wick create significant air quality constraints for development. Hackney Council's planning policies require detailed assessment of air quality impacts for most significant developments.

Air Dust Odour provides air quality assessments, dust management plans and odour impact assessments across the borough. As Chartered Environmentalists and MIAQM members, we prepare reports to the standards required by Hackney Council and the Greater London Authority.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need an air quality assessment in Hackney?

Hackney falls within the Greater London Air Quality Management Area and the ULEZ. Most planning applications for residential, commercial or mixed-use development will require an air quality assessment. The borough has a high density of sensitive receptors including schools, parks and residential properties near busy roads such as the A10, A107 and A102, and the council expects applicants to demonstrate that developments will not worsen local air quality or expose future occupants to unacceptable pollution levels.

What areas of Hackney do you cover?

We cover all areas of the London Borough of Hackney including Shoreditch, Dalston, Hackney Wick, Stoke Newington, Clapton, Homerton, Hoxton, Haggerston, Bethnal Green and surrounding neighbourhoods. We are familiar with local planning policy and the specific air quality challenges of each area.

Costs depend on the type and complexity of assessment required. Screening assessments typically start from around £500, detailed air quality assessments with dispersion modelling from around £1,500, and kitchen odour assessments from around £800. Contact us for a free, no-obligation quote tailored to your project.

Do I need a dust assessment for construction in Hackney?

Yes. Hackney Council requires a Construction Environmental Management Plan (CEMP) or dust assessment for most developments involving demolition, excavation or construction activities. This is particularly important in densely populated areas such as Shoreditch and Hackney Wick where sensitive receptors are in close proximity to development sites.

We are opening a restaurant in Hackney — do we need an odour assessment?

Yes, most new or significantly altered food premises in Hackney will require an odour impact assessment as part of the planning application. We prepare assessments in accordance with the EMAQ+ guidance on the assessment of odour from commercial kitchens. We can advise on the level of extraction required and help ensure your application meets council requirements.
