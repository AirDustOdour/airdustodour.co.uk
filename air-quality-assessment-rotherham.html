Air Quality Assessments in Rotherham

Expert air quality, dust and odour assessments across Rotherham and South Yorkshire. Our chartered professionals support planning applications throughout the borough.

Air Quality Specialists for Rotherham

Rotherham is a major South Yorkshire borough with one of England’s most distinctive industrial legacies: steelmaking and metal processing have shaped the landscape, economy and air quality of the borough for over two centuries. Rotherham Metropolitan Borough Council has declared Air Quality Management Areas covering the A630, sections of the M1 and the town centre, where nitrogen dioxide concentrations from heavy traffic on these strategic corridors have persistently exceeded the annual mean objective. But the borough’s air quality challenges extend beyond road traffic — the active steel processing operations at Templeborough and Parkgate generate dust and process odour that must be characterised and addressed in environmental assessments for development in surrounding areas.

Rotherham is experiencing a significant period of economic change. The Advanced Manufacturing Park at Waverley, adjacent to the M1 and close to the Sheffield boundary, has become one of the UK’s leading locations for aerospace and advanced engineering investment, attracting major employers and generating substantial new employment floorspace. The wider Waverley new community development — a large mixed-use scheme including thousands of new homes — sits directly alongside the AMP, creating a development environment where the interface between sensitive residential uses and nearby industrial operations requires particularly careful air quality and odour assessment. Across the wider borough, major housing growth is proposed in the Local Plan with significant brownfield sites coming forward for development.

At Air Dust Odour, we work with developers, architects and planning consultants on projects across Rotherham and South Yorkshire. Our Chartered Environmentalists bring specific expertise in industrial process odour and dust assessment alongside standard road traffic air quality work, and are familiar with Rotherham MBC’s technical requirements and planning conditions. We produce authoritative, clearly structured reports that satisfy planning officers and support successful outcomes. We provide the full range of environmental assessment services covering air quality, dust, odour, noise and lighting.

Our Services in Rotherham

We provide specialist air quality, dust and odour assessment services for planning applications across Rotherham and South Yorkshire.

Air Quality Assessment for Planning

Screening and detailed air quality assessments for residential, employment, mixed-use and industrial developments across the Rotherham borough. Our assessments address the requirements of Rotherham MBC including road traffic impact assessment, exposure assessments for residential development near AQMAs and motorway corridors, and the assessment of existing industrial source contributions where steel processing and heavy industrial operations form part of the baseline environment.

Construction Dust Assessment & CEMP

Dust risk assessments and Construction Environmental Management Plans for demolition, earthworks and construction across the borough. Rotherham’s steel industry heritage means that many brownfield sites contain legacy contamination and demolition material that requires careful management during construction to prevent dust and particulate matter affecting surrounding communities. Our CEMPs specify practical, enforceable mitigation in line with IAQM guidance and Rotherham MBC’s standard planning conditions.

Odour Assessment

Odour impact assessments for steel processing, industrial, waste and food operations across the borough, and receptor exposure assessments for residential development near existing odour sources. We characterise odour emissions using a combination of source data, site inspection and H4 methodology, applying quantitative dispersion modelling where the complexity of the source inventory or the sensitivity of proposed receptors requires it. We advise on mitigation technology and odour management plan content to support planning applications and Environment Agency permit applications.

Kitchen Odour Assessment (EMAQ Plus)

Kitchen odour risk assessments following the EMAQ+ methodology for restaurants, takeaways, cafes and commercial kitchens across Rotherham. Rotherham MBC routinely requires kitchen odour assessments for new or expanded commercial catering operations, particularly in Rotherham town centre and the district centres of Maltby, Wath-upon-Dearne and Wickersley, where residential amenity considerations make odour management a key planning issue.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need an air quality assessment in Rotherham?

If your development is within or adjacent to one of Rotherham Metropolitan Borough Council's Air Quality Management Areas, or introduces new sensitive receptors near a major road, an air quality assessment is very likely to be required. Rotherham's AQMAs cover the A630, sections of the M1 and the town centre, reflecting persistent exceedances of nitrogen dioxide objectives along these corridors. Residential, commercial and employment developments near these routes will typically need a detailed air quality assessment as a material part of the planning application.

How does Rotherham's steel and industrial heritage affect odour and dust assessments?

Rotherham's steel processing legacy at Templeborough and Parkgate creates a distinctive environmental assessment context. Active steelworks and metal processing operations in these areas generate dust and odour that must be characterised as part of any assessment for nearby residential or sensitive development, to demonstrate that future occupants will not be exposed to unacceptable impacts from existing industrial sources. Equally, developers of employment sites adjacent to steel processing operations may need to assess the cumulative contribution of their own operations to the local dust and odour environment, and Rotherham MBC will expect technically robust assessments that address the full range of existing and proposed sources.

What air quality considerations apply to the Advanced Manufacturing Park near Rotherham?

The Advanced Manufacturing Park at Waverley is a major employment site with ongoing construction activity and a growing number of high-technology manufacturing occupants. Developers bringing forward new plots on the AMP should consider both the air quality impacts of construction and the operational emissions from manufacturing processes, while developers of residential and sensitive uses nearby need to assess exposure to traffic from the employment park and any operational process emissions. Rotherham MBC takes a consistent approach to requiring air quality assessment for development in this growing employment corridor near the M1.

How much does an air quality assessment cost in Rotherham?

Costs depend on the type and complexity of assessment required. Screening assessments typically start from around £500, detailed air quality assessments with dispersion modelling from around £1,500, and odour assessments from around £800. Where the assessment must address both road traffic impacts and existing industrial sources such as steel processing operations, the scope and cost may be higher. How quickly can you deliver an air quality assessment in Rotherham?

A screening assessment can typically be completed within 5 to 10 working days. Detailed assessments incorporating dispersion modelling generally take 2 to 4 weeks, depending on project complexity, traffic data availability and whether industrial source characterisation is required. Where baseline air quality monitoring is needed, this can add 3 to 6 months. We are familiar with Rotherham MBC’s requirements and can work to tight planning application deadlines.
