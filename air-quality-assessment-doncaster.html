Air Quality Assessments in Doncaster

Expert air quality, dust and odour assessments across Doncaster and South Yorkshire. Our chartered professionals support planning applications throughout the borough.

Air Quality Specialists for Doncaster

Doncaster is one of the UK’s premier logistics and distribution locations, situated at the confluence of the M18, M1 and A1(M) — three of England’s most heavily trafficked strategic routes. Doncaster Metropolitan Borough Council has declared Air Quality Management Areas covering the A630, A18 and the city centre, where sustained HGV and commuter traffic generates persistent nitrogen dioxide exceedances. The borough’s flat topography and position on the River Don floodplain mean that air quality dispersion is generally more straightforward than in the South Yorkshire valleys, but the sheer scale of traffic movements associated with the logistics sector creates significant baseline pollution levels across wide areas of the district.

Doncaster is currently undergoing a substantial economic transformation. The former Doncaster Sheffield Airport site is being redeveloped as a major logistics, aerospace and advanced manufacturing hub — the South Yorkshire Investment Zone — generating significant new employment and a major uplift in HGV and workforce traffic. The iPort logistics park is already one of the largest rail-connected distribution centres in the UK. Alongside these large-scale employment developments, the borough has a substantial housing growth programme and a significant coalfield legacy, with former colliery and heavy industrial sites across the east of the borough requiring brownfield remediation where dust, odour and contamination are key environmental considerations.

At Air Dust Odour, we work with developers, architects, planning consultants and logistics operators across Doncaster and South Yorkshire. Our Chartered Environmentalists understand the specific technical demands of air quality assessment in this high-traffic, logistics-intensive environment, and are familiar with Doncaster MBC’s approach to air quality policy and planning conditions. We deliver clear, technically authoritative reports that keep your project on track, and provide the full range of associated environmental assessment services including dust, odour, noise and lighting.

What We Offer

Our Services in Doncaster

We provide specialist air quality, dust and odour assessment services for planning applications across Doncaster and South Yorkshire.

Air Quality Assessment for Planning

Screening and detailed air quality assessments for logistics, employment, residential and mixed-use developments across the Doncaster borough. Our assessments address the specific requirements of Doncaster MBC, including HGV traffic impact assessment for logistics developments, exposure assessments for new residential receptors near motorway and primary road corridors, and cumulative impact assessment for major development sites such as the South Yorkshire Investment Zone and iPort.

Construction Dust Assessment & CEMP

Dust risk assessments and Construction Environmental Management Plans for demolition, earthworks, remediation and construction across the borough. Doncaster’s coalfield legacy means many development sites involve brownfield land with extensive demolition and excavation requirements, and a robust dust assessment and CEMP are essential both to satisfy planning conditions and to manage the risk of dust nuisance and contamination spread during the construction phase.

Odour Assessment

Odour impact assessments for industrial, waste, agricultural and food processing operations across the borough. We apply IAQM guidance and the EA’s H4 methodology to evaluate odour emissions, dispersion and receptor exposure, providing quantitative dispersion modelling where the complexity of the source or the proximity of sensitive receptors requires a higher level of technical analysis. We advise on abatement technology and support the preparation of odour management plans.

Kitchen Odour Assessment (EMAQ Plus)

Kitchen odour risk assessments following the EMAQ+ methodology for restaurants, takeaways, cafes and commercial kitchens across Doncaster. Doncaster MBC routinely requires kitchen odour assessments for new or altered commercial catering operations in the city centre and across the borough’s district centres, particularly where the proposed flue discharge point is in proximity to residential windows or habitable spaces above ground floor level.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need an air quality assessment in Doncaster?

If your development is within or adjacent to one of Doncaster Metropolitan Borough Council's Air Quality Management Areas, or introduces new sensitive receptors near a major road, an air quality assessment is very likely to be required. Doncaster's AQMAs cover the A630, A18 and the city centre, reflecting nitrogen dioxide exceedances along these heavily trafficked corridors. Residential, commercial and logistics developments near these routes, or in proximity to the major motorway junctions at the M18/M1/A1(M) intersection, will typically need a detailed air quality assessment to accompany the planning application.

What air quality assessment requirements apply to the iPort and Great Yorkshire Way logistics developments?

The redevelopment of the former Doncaster Sheffield Airport site and the surrounding iPort logistics campus is generating substantial new HGV traffic across the borough, and air quality assessment is a material consideration for both individual development plots and the cumulative effects of the wider scheme. New logistics, employment and associated residential development in this corridor must address HGV diesel emissions, the dispersion of nitrogen dioxide and particulate matter towards existing settlements, and the contribution of the development to the borough-wide traffic network. Doncaster MBC expects developers to engage proactively with air quality modelling at the pre-application stage for major schemes in this area.

How does Doncaster's coalfield legacy affect dust assessment requirements?

Significant areas of the Doncaster borough overlie the South Yorkshire coalfield, and many development sites involve former colliery, coking and heavy industrial land with the potential for extensive demolition, excavation and remediation generating substantial dust. A construction dust risk assessment and Construction Environmental Management Plan are routinely required by Doncaster MBC for brownfield development schemes, with particular attention paid to the proximity of residential receptors and the potential for contaminated material to be disturbed and transported off-site as dust. Our assessments follow IAQM guidance and specify proportionate, enforceable mitigation measures.

Are air quality assessments required for HS2-related development in Doncaster?

The HS2 route passes through parts of the Doncaster borough and associated infrastructure works, station development and residential relocations all carry air quality assessment implications. Construction of HS2 infrastructure is accompanied by a comprehensive Environmental Statement, but developers bringing forward enabling development or schemes in proximity to the route should consider carefully whether cumulative air quality impacts need to be addressed in their own planning documentation. We advise clients on the scope of assessment required and how to address HS2 construction impacts in planning submissions to Doncaster MBC.

How quickly can you deliver an air quality assessment in Doncaster?

A screening assessment can typically be completed within 5 to 10 working days. Detailed assessments incorporating dispersion modelling generally take 2 to 4 weeks, depending on project complexity and the availability of traffic data. Where baseline air quality monitoring is required, this can add 3 to 6 months to the programme. We are experienced in Doncaster MBC’s requirements and can work to planning application deadlines, including expedited turnaround where the programme demands it.
