COSHH dust monitoring for Colchester employers

Colchester has a broad industrial and construction economy. Every workplace generating airborne dust has a legal duty under COSHH Regulation 10 to measure worker exposure to airborne hazardous substances and to compare the result to the Workplace Exposure Limits in HSE document EH40.

HSE East and South East regularly inspects joineries, food premises and metal fabrication workshops across Essex. We provide UKAS-accredited personal IOM-head inhalable and cyclone respirable sampling for Colchester employers, plus direct-reading gas monitoring where relevant.

At Air Dust Odour we carry out personal breathing-zone sampling and fixed static monitoring across Essex. Filters are sent to a UKAS-accredited laboratory and every result is compared to the relevant Workplace Exposure Limit in HSE document EH40. Surveys are planned and signed off by Malcolm Pounder CEnv MIAQM, a Chartered Environmentalist with two decades of hands-on air-quality experience.

Wood Dust Monitoring (IOM head)

Personal sampling of hardwood and softwood dust at Colchester joineries, sawmills, kitchen-fitter workshops and on-site carpentry. WEL 3 mg/m³ 8-hr TWA — one of the lowest dust limits in EH40 and frequently exceeded.

Inhalable & Respirable Dust

Flour, Grain & Food Dusts

Inhalable food dusts including flour (WEL 10 mg/m³) and grain (WEL 10 mg/m³). Important for Colchester bakeries, food manufacturers, animal-feed mills and grain stores.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need workplace exposure monitoring in Colchester?

If your Colchester site generates dust, fume or vapour from processes such as engineering, joinery, food production, construction or stone and concrete cutting, you have a legal duty under COSHH Regulation 10 to assess and control worker exposure. Where there is no obviously low-risk exposure, monitoring with personal sampling pumps is normally the only defensible way to demonstrate compliance with the relevant Workplace Exposure Limit in HSE document EH40. Colchester sits within HSE East and South East HSE jurisdiction, and inspectors increasingly expect to see recent, UKAS-analysed personal sampling data on file.

Which industries in Colchester most commonly need dust monitoring?

Colchester's economy spans construction, the University, joinery and the garrison economy. The most commonly monitored substances are wood dust in joineries (WEL 3 mg/m³), respirable crystalline silica in stonework and concrete cutting (WEL 0.1 mg/m³), inhalable mixed dust in light manufacturing, and welding fume in metal fabrication (HSE Group 1 carcinogen since 2019).

How quickly can you survey a Colchester site?

For most Colchester sites we can usually be on site within 2 to 3 weeks of a confirmed booking, with sampling carried out over a full representative shift. Filters are dispatched same-day to a UKAS-accredited laboratory; results typically take 5 to 10 working days, and the written report follows within a further week. Where HSE has issued an Improvement Notice we can usually expedite the visit.

Do you cover Colchester for COSHH wood dust and silica monitoring specifically?

Yes. Wood dust (hardwood and softwood, WEL 3 mg/m³ inhalable) and respirable crystalline silica (WEL 0.1 mg/m³) are two of the most commonly monitored substances on Colchester sites — particularly in joineries, kitchen-fitter workshops, stone masonries, engineered-quartz worktop fabricators, and any construction or demolition site cutting concrete or brick. We carry both IOM heads and Higgins-Dewell cyclones on every visit and can combine the two sampling regimes on the same shift.
