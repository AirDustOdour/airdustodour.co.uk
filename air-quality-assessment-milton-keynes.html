Environmental Consultants in Milton Keynes

Milton Keynes is one of the UK's fastest-growing cities, with ambitious plans for continued expansion and tens of thousands of new homes planned across the borough over the coming decades. Its unique grid road network and planned urban structure continue to evolve, with significant development taking place in areas such as Tattenhoe Park, Brooklands and the Eastern Expansion Area.

Despite its reputation for green spaces and modern planning, Milton Keynes has declared Air Quality Management Areas where national objectives for nitrogen dioxide are not met. Key road corridors including the A5, A509 and Central Milton Keynes grid roads are the primary sources of traffic-related air pollution. The council expects air quality to be considered in planning applications for developments near these routes or that generate significant additional traffic.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need an air quality assessment in Milton Keynes?

Milton Keynes Council has declared an Air Quality Management Area covering parts of the town, particularly around the A5, A509 and the Central Milton Keynes grid roads. Most planning applications for residential, commercial or mixed-use development in or near an AQMA will require an air quality assessment. Even outside the AQMA, major developments may still require assessment if they generate significant traffic or are located near busy roads.

What are the main air quality concerns in Milton Keynes?

Despite its planned layout and extensive green space, Milton Keynes has air quality issues concentrated on its major road corridors including the A5 Watling Street, A509, H5 and V6 grid roads. The town's rapid population growth and reliance on the car have led to traffic-related nitrogen dioxide exceedances in parts of the town centre and along key routes.

How much does an air quality assessment cost in Milton Keynes?

Costs depend on the type and complexity of assessment required. Screening assessments typically start from around £500, detailed air quality assessments with dispersion modelling from around £1,500, and kitchen odour assessments from around £800. Do I need a dust assessment for construction in Milton Keynes?

Yes. Milton Keynes Council requires a Construction Environmental Management Plan (CEMP) or dust assessment for most significant developments involving demolition, excavation or construction near sensitive receptors. We prepare CEMPs in accordance with IAQM guidance on the assessment of dust from demolition and construction.

We are opening a restaurant or takeaway in Milton Keynes — do we need an odour assessment?

Yes, most new or significantly altered food premises in Milton Keynes will require an odour impact assessment as part of the planning application, particularly where residential properties are nearby. We prepare assessments in accordance with EMAQ+ guidance and can advise on appropriate extraction and filtration systems.
