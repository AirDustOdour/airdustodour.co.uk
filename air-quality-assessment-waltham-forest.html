Environmental Consultants in Waltham Forest

Waltham Forest spans from the urban neighbourhoods of Leyton and Walthamstow in the south to the leafy suburbs of Chingford in the north, bordering Epping Forest. The borough has seen significant regeneration investment and residential development pressure, particularly around Walthamstow town centre and the Blackhorse Lane area.

The borough sits within the Greater London Air Quality Management Area and the Ultra Low Emission Zone. Major road corridors including the North Circular (A406), A112 and A104 create air quality constraints for development close to these routes. Waltham Forest Council's planning policies require assessment of air quality impacts in line with Greater London Authority guidance.

Air Dust Odour provides air quality assessments, dust management plans and odour impact assessments across the borough. As Chartered Environmentalists and MIAQM members, we prepare reports to the standards required by Waltham Forest Council and the GLA.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need an air quality assessment in Waltham Forest?

Waltham Forest falls within the Greater London Air Quality Management Area and the ULEZ. Most planning applications for residential, commercial or mixed-use development will require an air quality assessment. Key roads including the A112, A104 and North Circular create air quality constraints for development, and the council expects applicants to demonstrate that proposed developments will not worsen local air quality or expose future occupants to unacceptable pollution.

What areas of Waltham Forest do you cover?

We cover all areas of the London Borough of Waltham Forest including Walthamstow, Leyton, Leytonstone, Chingford, Highams Park, Woodford Green and surrounding neighbourhoods. We are familiar with the borough's planning policies and air quality requirements.

How much does an air quality assessment cost in Waltham Forest?

Costs depend on the type and complexity of the assessment required. Screening assessments typically start from around £500, detailed air quality assessments with dispersion modelling from around £1,500, and kitchen odour assessments from around £800. Contact us for a free, no-obligation quote tailored to your project.

Do I need a dust assessment for construction in Waltham Forest?

Yes. Waltham Forest Council requires a Construction Environmental Management Plan (CEMP) or dust assessment for most developments involving demolition, excavation or construction in sensitive areas. We prepare CEMPs in line with the IAQM guidance on the assessment of dust from demolition and construction.

We are opening a food business in Waltham Forest — do we need an odour assessment?

Yes, most new or significantly altered food premises in Waltham Forest will require an odour impact assessment as part of the planning application. We prepare assessments in accordance with EMAQ+ guidance and can advise on appropriate extraction and filtration systems to meet the council's requirements.
