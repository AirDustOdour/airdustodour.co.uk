Air Quality Assessments in Havering

Expert air quality, dust and odour assessments across the London Borough of Havering. Our chartered professionals support planning applications for Romford town centre regeneration, Beam Park development and residential schemes throughout the borough.

Air Quality Specialists for Havering

The London Borough of Havering covers a large area of outer east London, stretching from Romford town centre to the Thames marshes at Rainham and the semi-rural fringe near Upminster and Cranham. Air Quality Management Areas have been declared across Romford town centre and along key arterial routes including the A12 Eastern Avenue, A127 Southend Arterial Road and A1306 New Road, where road traffic emissions have historically produced elevated nitrogen dioxide concentrations.

Havering is experiencing a significant wave of development activity driven by the Romford town centre regeneration programme — one of outer London's most ambitious, with thousands of new homes, a transformed station quarter and new commercial and leisure facilities planned — and the major Beam Park development on the former Ford Dunton works site in Rainham, which will deliver several thousand additional homes alongside commercial and community uses. Together with ongoing residential development across Hornchurch, Upminster, Harold Hill and the borough's suburban areas, this makes air quality assessment expertise a consistent requirement throughout Havering.

At Air Dust Odour, we work with developers, architects and planning consultants across Havering and the wider east London area. Our MIAQM-qualified professionals understand the requirements of Havering Council and the Greater London Authority, and deliver technically robust assessments that address the specific air quality challenges of this diverse borough.

What We Offer

Our Services in Havering

We provide specialist air quality, dust and odour assessment services for planning applications across the London Borough of Havering.

Air Quality Assessment for Planning

Screening and detailed air quality assessments for residential, commercial and mixed-use developments across Havering. Our assessments follow IAQM guidance and London Plan policy, addressing ULEZ context, AQMA designations and the requirements of Havering Council, including Air Quality Neutral assessment for major schemes and thorough exposure assessment for new residential receptors near the A12, A127 and A1306.

Construction Dust Assessment & CEMP

Dust risk assessments and Construction Environmental Management Plans for demolition, earthworks and construction across Havering. The scale of development at Beam Park and in Romford town centre means construction dust management is a key planning consideration, and a robust CEMP is essential to satisfy Havering Council planning conditions and protect neighbouring residents and businesses throughout the construction programme.

Odour Assessment

Odour assessments for residential developments near industrial uses and for commercial operations requiring planning consent in Havering. The industrial and waste management land uses around Rainham and along the Thames corridor mean odour can be a material consideration for new residential development in the south of the borough, and we provide assessments in line with Environment Agency H4 guidance.

Kitchen Odour Assessment (EMAQ Plus)

Odour risk assessments for restaurants, takeaways, cafes and commercial kitchens using the EMAQ+ methodology. Romford's expanding town centre and the new mixed-use development in the borough regularly generate requirements for kitchen odour assessments, particularly where catering uses are proposed in mixed-use buildings with residential accommodation above.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need an air quality assessment in Havering?

Most developments in Havering requiring planning permission will need at least a screening-level air quality assessment. The borough has declared Air Quality Management Areas along key road corridors including the A12, A127 and A1306 and around Romford town centre. The expanded ULEZ applies throughout Havering, and London Plan policies require major developments to formally address air quality impacts including Air Quality Neutral assessment for large schemes.

What are the air quality requirements for the Romford regeneration programme?

Romford town centre is the focus of one of outer London's most ambitious regeneration programmes, with thousands of new homes and significant commercial uses planned. The town centre sits within an AQMA and adjacent to the A12 and A118. Developments in Romford typically require detailed air quality assessments, Air Quality Neutral assessment for major schemes, construction dust assessments, and odour assessments where commercial kitchen uses are proposed in mixed-use buildings.

What air quality issues apply to Beam Park development in Havering?

Beam Park is a major mixed-use development on the former Ford Dunton site in Rainham, delivering several thousand new homes. The site sits adjacent to the A1306 and near the A13, two of east London's busiest road corridors. Air quality assessments for Beam Park need to address elevated nitrogen dioxide from these roads, potential cumulative impacts, and dust management during extensive earthworks. Industrial uses in the Rainham area may also be relevant for odour assessment.

How much does an air quality assessment cost in Havering?

Costs depend on the scope and complexity of the assessment. Screening assessments typically start from around £500, detailed assessments with dispersion modelling from around £1,500, and kitchen odour assessments from around £800. For major schemes near the A12, A127 or at Beam Park, more detailed analysis is typically required. Contact us for a free, no-obligation quote tailored to your project.

How quickly can you deliver an air quality assessment in Havering?

A screening assessment can typically be completed within 5 to 10 working days. Detailed assessments with dispersion modelling usually take 2 to 4 weeks depending on project complexity and traffic data availability. If baseline air quality monitoring is required, this can extend the programme by 3 to 6 months. We work to your project timeline and can offer expedited turnaround where needed.
