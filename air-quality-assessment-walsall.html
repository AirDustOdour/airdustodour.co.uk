Air Quality Specialists for Walsall

Walsall is a Black Country borough of significant industrial character and substantial development ambition, situated immediately north-east of Birmingham and connected to the wider West Midlands conurbation by an extensive road and rail network. The borough has a strong tradition of metal manufacturing, leather goods production and, more recently, logistics and distribution, and this industrial legacy shapes the environmental baseline against which all new development must be assessed. Significant housing growth is planned across the borough, including major allocations at Darlaston and Brownhills, bringing with it a corresponding requirement for thorough environmental assessment.

Walsall Council has declared Air Quality Management Areas covering the A34, A461 and the town centre, where nitrogen dioxide and PM10 concentrations have exceeded national objectives. Beyond these declared AQMAs, the M6 motorway passes through the borough via Junctions 7 to 10, generating elevated background pollution levels across a broad swathe of the urban area and creating challenging conditions for sensitive development proposals in the motorway corridor. Walsall has some of the most difficult air quality in the West Midlands outside of Birmingham city centre, and planning officers expect technically rigorous assessments that reflect this challenging baseline.

At Air Dust Odour, we understand the specific environmental context of Walsall and the wider Black Country. Our Chartered Environmentalists and Members of the Institute of Air Quality Management deliver assessments that are proportionate to the scale of the project, technically robust in their methodology, and written in plain language that planning officers can act upon. Whether you are bringing forward a residential scheme on a former industrial site, opening a new food business in the town centre, or seeking discharge of a planning condition, we have the expertise to support your application from inception to approval.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need an air quality assessment near the M6 in Walsall?

Yes. The M6 motorway passes through the heart of Walsall borough, and the elevated background concentrations of nitrogen dioxide and particulate matter in its corridor mean that any development introducing sensitive receptors — such as residential properties, schools or care facilities — near Junctions 7 to 10 will require a robust air quality assessment. Walsall Council expects applicants to quantify the impact of roadside pollution on future occupants, and planning officers are well-versed in the specific challenges posed by motorway proximity. Dispersion modelling is typically required for sites within 200 metres of the M6 carriageway.

What industrial odour issues are common in Walsall?

Walsall's long tradition of metal manufacturing, leather goods production and more recent logistics and waste management activity means that odour is a recurring planning consideration across many parts of the borough. Proposals for residential development near active or historic industrial sites frequently require an odour impact assessment to demonstrate that future occupants will not be exposed to unacceptable odour levels from neighbouring operations. Equally, businesses wishing to establish or expand operations that may generate odour — including food processing, waste handling and certain manufacturing processes — are expected to demonstrate that their emissions can be adequately controlled.

Do I need a CEMP for brownfield development in Walsall?

Most brownfield development projects in Walsall that require planning permission will be required to submit a Construction Environmental Management Plan as a planning condition. Walsall's industrial legacy means that many development sites involve demolition of existing structures, remediation of contaminated ground, and extensive earthworks — all of which generate dust and can mobilise contaminated particulates if not properly managed. Walsall Council follows IAQM guidance in its approach to construction dust, and a thorough CEMP addressing dust control, site management and monitoring is a standard requirement for anything beyond minor development.

We are opening a food business in Walsall — do we need an odour assessment?

New food businesses involving commercial cooking — including restaurants, takeaways, bakeries and hot food counters — are very likely to require an odour assessment as part of the planning application in Walsall. Walsall Council's environmental health officers require applicants to demonstrate that cooking odour emissions can be adequately controlled, typically through an EMAQ+ assessment that evaluates the separation distance between the extraction point and the nearest sensitive receptors, the odour generation rate of the proposed cuisine type, and the effectiveness of proposed abatement equipment. Early engagement with a specialist consultant at the design stage is strongly recommended.

How much does an air quality assessment cost in Walsall?

Costs are project-specific and depend on the type of assessment required and the complexity of the site and its surroundings. Screening assessments typically start from around £500, detailed air quality assessments with dispersion modelling from around £1,500, and EMAQ+ kitchen odour assessments from around £800. Projects near the M6 corridor or within Walsall's declared AQMAs may require more detailed modelling, which is reflected in the fee. We offer free, no-obligation quotes tailored to your project.
