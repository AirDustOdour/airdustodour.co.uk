What We Provide

Our Services for Planning Consultants

The full range of air quality, dust and odour assessment services — everything your planning application is likely to need, from one trusted subconsultant.

Air Quality Assessment

Screening and detailed air quality assessments for planning applications of all sizes and types. We are experienced with the requirements of local authorities across England, Wales, Scotland and Northern Ireland, and we write reports that planning officers and environmental health teams are satisfied with — reliably, on the first submission.

Dust & CEMP

Dust risk assessments and Construction Environmental Management Plans prepared to the IAQM methodology. We know exactly what different planning authorities expect in a CEMP and we prepare them accordingly — including site-specific mitigation measures, monitoring protocols and the responsible person structure that planning officers need to see.

Odour Assessment

EMAQ+ kitchen odour assessments for food and drink uses, IAQM odour assessments for industrial and waste development, and odour dispersion modelling for permit applications. We are the go-to subconsultant for planning consultancies handling food premises applications — fast, technically sound and familiar with what councils require.

Expert Witness & Appeal Support

Where an air quality, dust or odour matter goes to appeal, we provide expert witness services, prepare proofs of evidence, and attend hearings or inquiries as required. Our MIAQM and CEnv credentials are recognised by the Planning Inspectorate as evidence of expert competence in the field.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Do you work as a subconsultant to planning consultancies?

Yes — working as a subconsultant to planning consultancies is a core part of what we do. We understand the importance of operating as a trusted partner rather than a competitor, and we are completely comfortable working in the background. We engage with the planning authority on technical matters when required, but we always communicate through you so you remain in control of the client relationship. We are discreet, professional and focused entirely on making your project a success.

How quickly can you turn around an air quality report?

For straightforward screening assessments, we can typically turn around a draft report within 5 working days. Detailed assessments with dispersion modelling usually take 2 to 4 weeks, depending on site complexity and data requirements. We are experienced at working to tight planning timescales and can discuss expedited turnaround where your programme demands it. We always agree a clear delivery date upfront and deliver on time.

What credentials do you hold?

Our team holds MIAQM (Member of the Institute of Air Quality Management) and CEnv (Chartered Environmentalist) accreditation. These are the principal professional credentials for air quality practitioners in the UK and are recognised by planning authorities and the Planning Inspectorate as evidence of competence. We maintain our continuing professional development in line with IAQM requirements and stay current with evolving guidance and policy.

Can you respond to planning authority queries on our behalf?

Yes. Responding to planning authority comments and queries — from environmental health officers, planning officers or third-party consultees — is included within our agreed fee as standard. We draft responses to technical queries, prepare supplementary technical notes where needed, and liaise directly with the LPA's environmental health team when it is helpful to do so. Our aim is always to resolve queries at the first attempt so your application keeps moving forward.

Do you cover the whole of the UK?

Yes — we work on projects across England, Wales, Scotland and Northern Ireland. We are familiar with the variations in local planning authority requirements across different regions and nations, including the distinct policy frameworks that apply in Wales (Future Wales, TAN policies) and Scotland (NPF4). Whatever the location of your project, we have the knowledge to prepare an assessment that meets local requirements and is accepted by the relevant authority.
