Environmental Consultants in Stockport

Stockport Metropolitan Borough stretches south of Manchester, straddling the M60 orbital motorway and encompassing a diverse range of environments from the dense urban core of Stockport town centre to the leafier suburban areas of Bramhall, Marple and Romiley. The borough is undergoing a sustained period of development activity, with Stockport town centre at the heart of a major regeneration programme designed to deliver thousands of new homes and commercial floorspace over the coming decade. This combination of legacy air quality pressures and growing development demand makes professional environmental assessment an essential part of the planning process across the borough.

Stockport Metropolitan Borough Council has designated AQMAs covering the M60, the A6, the A560 and the town centre, all of which fall within the wider Greater Manchester Air Quality Management Area (GMAQMA). The M60 motorway is the single largest air quality source in the borough, generating a substantial nitrogen dioxide and PM2.5 footprint across communities including Reddish, Heaton Mersey and Cheadle. Along the A6 and A560, elevated road traffic concentrations affect planning-sensitive receptors including residential properties, schools and care homes that sit close to these busy corridors. Any development that brings new residential use within range of these sources requires careful assessment of exposure and, where necessary, specification of mitigation measures such as building orientation, ventilation design or glazing specifications.

Our Chartered Environmentalists are familiar with the council's local planning policies and its approach to air quality conditions, and we work efficiently to deliver the technically robust assessments needed to progress applications. Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need an air quality assessment in Stockport?

Stockport Metropolitan Borough Council has declared AQMAs covering the M60 orbital motorway, the A6, the A560 and Stockport town centre, all of which sit within the broader Greater Manchester Air Quality Management Area. Planning applications for residential, commercial or mixed-use developments in or near these designations will routinely require an air quality assessment. The council expects applicants to demonstrate that proposed developments will not worsen pollution levels or expose future occupants to concentrations above the national air quality objectives.

Why is the M60 such an important air quality factor in Stockport?

The M60 orbital motorway passes through the heart of Stockport borough and is the dominant source of nitrogen dioxide and particulate matter in the area. Its high traffic volumes, including a significant proportion of heavy goods vehicles, generate a persistent pollution corridor that affects residential and commercial properties in Reddish, Heaton Mersey, Cheadle and other neighbourhoods adjacent to the motorway. Developers proposing sensitive uses within several hundred metres of the M60 should expect detailed air quality assessment to be required.

Screening assessments typically start from around £500, detailed assessments with dispersion modelling from around £1,500, and kitchen odour assessments from around £800. Projects near the M60 or within the town centre regeneration area may require more extensive modelling given the complexity of local sources. Is a dust assessment required for the Stockport town centre regeneration?

Yes. Stockport town centre is undergoing major regeneration with significant new residential and commercial development planned, and Stockport Council requires dust risk assessments and Construction Environmental Management Plans for most schemes involving demolition or significant groundworks in this densely occupied area. We prepare CEMPs in line with IAQM guidance and ensure that dust mitigation is proportionate to the scale and sensitivity of the development context.

What development areas in Stockport most commonly need air quality reports?

The M60 corridor, Stockport town centre, Heaton Mersey and Cheadle are the areas where air quality assessment requests are most frequent, driven by a combination of AQMA designations, proximity to major roads and significant development activity. Hazel Grove and Romiley are also seeing increased residential development where road proximity triggers assessment requirements. We can advise quickly on whether your specific site and proposal are likely to need assessment.
